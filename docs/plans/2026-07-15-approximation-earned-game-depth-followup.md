## Why this follow-up exists

The published A3 chapter was scientifically careful and visually polished, but its original completion controls were self-attested and several laboratories behaved primarily as reactive instruments. The delayed learning-depth audit classified only a minority of the twelve laboratories as genuine multi-stage games.

This follow-up preserves the route, scientific models, ten plots, and all twelve canonical mission IDs while replacing self-attestation with earned evidence. Each laboratory now follows the same research loop:

1. orient to a bounded claim;
2. predict or choose before the decisive evidence is shown;
3. commit a consequential decision;
4. inspect a diagnostic or withheld oracle;
5. recover from an incorrect attempt;
6. interpret what remains omitted;
7. earn a workshop seal only after the objective is demonstrated.

## Persistence and compatibility

- Canonical Academy progress remains under the existing Academy progress store and canonical mission IDs.
- Chapter game evidence is stored separately under `project-xc-approximation-games-v2`.
- Reload restores cleared cases, notebook evidence, and seal gates.
- Previously completed canonical missions remain completed and usable even when they predate the deeper notebook schema; no historical progress is revoked.
- Chapter reset removes only A3 Academy missions and A3 game evidence, then re-locks all A3 oracles/helpers/readouts and restores fixed dossier controls. It does not alter Foundations, Mathematical Language, Atomic Structure, or Basis Set Quest state.
- Malformed or unavailable local storage falls back to a clean game state without blocking the chapter.

## Scientific claim boundaries retained

- Variational and Ritz claims are finite-model statements under the stated admissibility and nesting hypotheses.
- The six-site Ritz oracle is not a continuum or complete-basis limit.
- Perturbative trust is measured against this laboratory’s finite two-level model and is not a molecular theorem.
- Asymptotic “best order” is relative to the displayed finite range and documented numerical reference.
- Error components are signed deterministic differences, not statistical uncertainties.
- A Temple-style lower estimate is shown only when the declared spectral-separation hypothesis is satisfied.
- An exact no-preferred-basis claim is reserved for an actual scalar perturbation matrix. A tiny nonzero splitting is reported as numerically unresolved.
- Coincident generalized roots render as one physical rail with separated labels rather than two unreadable overprinted rails.
