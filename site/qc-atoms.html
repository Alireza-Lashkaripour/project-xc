Professor mode · exact within a model is not exact for nature

An analytic eigenpair may exactly solve a simplified Hamiltonian while that Hamiltonian omits finite nuclear mass, relativistic, radiative, external-field, and many-electron effects. Numerical error and model error are different axes; neither should be hidden inside the word “exact.”

Level 2 · Deep game 2 · Quantum Number Gate

Three integers organize one-electron spatial states

\[ \begin{aligned} n&=1,2,\ldots,\\ l&=0,\ldots,n-1,\\ m&=-l,\ldots,+l. \end{aligned} \]

The Coulomb eigenfunction separates as \(\psi_{nlm}(r,\theta,\phi)=R_{nl}(r)Y_l^m(\theta,\phi)\). The node ledger is exact for these eigenfunctions: radial nodes \(n-l-1\), angular nodes \(l\), and total nodes \(n-1\).

Degenerate m corridor

Each spatial orbital can hold two electrons only after spin is included. The \(2n^2\) shell capacity is therefore not the number of spatial wavefunctions; the spatial degeneracy is \(n^2\).

Picture warning: a familiar real \(p_x\) or \(d_{xy}\) picture is generally a real combination of complex \(m\) eigenfunctions. Do not assign a unique nonzero \(m\) to a real lobe drawing.
Professor mode · degeneracy is a subspace statement

For the ideal Coulomb Hamiltonian, energy depends only on \(n\), so all \(l,m\) values inside one shell are degenerate. The Hamiltonian does not prefer one basis inside an exactly degenerate subspace. External fields, fine structure, or many-electron interactions can reduce that degeneracy.

Level 3 · Deep game 3 · Coulomb Energy Ladder

Hydrogenic energy knows n and Z—but not l or m

\[ \begin{aligned} \hat H&=-\frac12\nabla^2-\frac{Z}{r},\\ E_n&=-\frac{Z^2}{2n^2}\ E_{\mathrm h}. \end{aligned} \]
Professor mode · what breaks same-n degeneracy

The accidental Coulomb degeneracy reflects more symmetry than ordinary rotational invariance. Finite nuclear mass changes the scale; relativistic and radiative corrections distinguish some states; electron–electron interactions and screening create strong l dependence in many-electron atoms.

Level 4 · Deep game 4 · Radial Expedition

A radial wavefunction can change sign while probability stays nonnegative

In atomic units, with \(r\) measured in \(a_0\) and \(\rho=2Zr/n\), the normalized radial function is

\[ \begin{aligned} \rho&=\frac{2Zr}{n},\\ \mathcal N_{nl}^{2}&=\left(\frac{2Z}{n}\right)^3\\ &\quad\times\frac{(n-l-1)!}{2n(n+l)!},\\ R_{nl}(r)&=\mathcal N_{nl}e^{-\rho/2}\rho^lL_{n-l-1}^{2l+1}(\rho),\\ P(r)&=r^2|R_{nl}(r)|^2. \end{aligned} \]
Density warning: \(|R(r)|^2\) alone is not the radial probability density. The spherical volume element contributes \(r^2\), so the probability of finding the electron between \(r\) and \(r+dr\) is \(r^2|R(r)|^2dr\).
Professor mode · numerical audit

The production model evaluates generalized Laguerre polynomials by recurrence, brackets every positive Laguerre zero, and checks a trapezoidal finite-domain normalization. Node counts are analytic; displayed node radii and normalization are deterministic numerical diagnostics.

Level 5 · Deep game 5 · Orbital Node Studio

Orbital pictures encode phase sign and nodes—not little electron balloons

For visual chemistry, real tesseral combinations are often more useful than complex \(Y_l^m\). The studio draws sign and exact angular zeros of functions proportional to \(x/r\), \(xy/r^2\), \((x^2-y^2)/r^2\), or \((2z^2-x^2-y^2)/r^2\).

Professor mode · rotations inside degenerate subspaces

Real p and d orbitals are basis choices in degenerate angular subspaces. Rotating those shapes changes the basis, not the Coulomb energy. In a molecule or external field, the surrounding Hamiltonian can select preferred combinations and orientations.

Level 6 · Deep game 6 · Spectral Detective

A line needs an energy difference and a transition mechanism

For an electric-dipole angular factor in the orbital-only model, the familiar rules are \(\Delta l=\pm1\) and \(\Delta m=0,\pm1\). The game separately reports the Coulomb energy difference and this angular filter:

\[ \begin{aligned} \Delta E&=E_f-E_i,\\ \lambda&=\frac{hc}{|\Delta E|}. \end{aligned} \]

Professor mode · why model and experiment differ

The chapter uses infinite-mass Coulomb differences. Reduced mass shifts even hydrogenic wavelengths; fine, hyperfine, Lamb, isotope, and field effects add structure. Use NIST ASD for evaluated lines and transition probabilities.

Level 7 · Deep game 7 · Shielding Arena

Electron–electron repulsion destroys the simple Coulomb shell story

A many-electron atomic Hamiltonian contains pair repulsions \(1/r_{ij}\). No independent \(-Z_{\mathrm{eff}}/r\) potential is exact for the interacting problem. Slater’s historical rules instead provide a transparent, named screening estimate:

\[ \begin{aligned} Z_{\mathrm{eff}}&=Z-S,\\ E_{\mathrm{screened}}^{\mathrm{toy}}&=-\frac{Z_{\mathrm{eff}}^2}{2n^2}. \end{aligned} \]

Every contribution exposed

Compare C 2p, Na 3s, and Fe 3d. The rule groups electrons differently for s/p and d targets; the numbers are an approximation with a provenance, not fitted browser decoration.

Do not over-interpret Zeff: the displayed toy energy is neither a measured ionization energy nor a generally valid Hartree–Fock or Kohn–Sham orbital energy. Electron correlation and orbital relaxation are outside this scalar rule.
Professor mode · central fields and antisymmetry

Useful independent-particle pictures replace the pair problem by effective one-electron equations, but the total wavefunction must still satisfy fermionic antisymmetry. Mean fields, exchange, correlation, and configuration interaction are distinct layers—not synonyms for “shielding.”

Professor mode · configuration is not a wavefunction solution

A configuration specifies occupations of chosen one-electron orbitals. It can support many determinants, spin-adapted functions, and terms. A single occupation string does not provide radial orbitals, energies, mixing coefficients, correlation, or a unique many-electron state.

Level 9 · Deep game 9 · Periodic Pattern Detective

Recover period, block, and unpaired-electron patterns from the ledger

Periodic trends begin with shell structure but do not end there. This game asks only claims that the stated neutral H–Kr configuration model can audit. It does not manufacture smooth radius or ionization-energy curves from a simple rule.

Professor mode · what an experiment measures

Atomic radii depend on operational definition, and ionization energies compare total energies of different charge states. Neither observable is exactly a one-orbital picture. Use evaluated data and report the definition before interpreting a periodic trend.

Level 10 · Deep game 10 · Microstate Foundry

Term symbols emerge by exhausting the determinant microstate table

For \(q\) equivalent electrons in a subshell \(l\), choose \(q\) spin orbitals from \(2(2l+1)\). Every determinant contributes one \((M_L,M_S)\) cell. The foundry repeatedly removes complete LS rectangles to obtain allowed terms \(^{2S+1}L\).

\[ \begin{aligned} g&=2(2l+1),\\ N_{\mathrm{det}}&=\binom{g}{q},\\ \dim({}^{2S+1}L)&=(2S+1)(2L+1). \end{aligned} \]
Term warning: \(^{2S+1}L\) is a many-electron angular-momentum label, not an orbital label. A p² configuration can produce several terms because different antisymmetrized combinations have different total L and S.
Professor mode · repeated terms and parentage

For larger equivalent-electron configurations, the same LS label can occur more than once. The production decomposition reports multiplicity of repeated labels, but does not assign seniority, coefficients of fractional parentage, electrostatic energies, or level mixing.

Level 11 · Deep game 11 · Fine-Structure Ladder

Couple L and S, then audit every J level and its barycenter

In the ideal pure-LS model with a supplied constant \(A\),

\[ \begin{aligned} J&=|L-S|,\ldots,L+S,\\ \Delta E_J&=\frac{A}{2}\left[J(J+1)\right.\\ &\qquad\left.-L(L+1)-S(S+1)\right]. \end{aligned} \]
Coupling warning: LS coupling is a regime, not a universal identity. Intermediate coupling and configuration interaction can mix labels; hyperfine structure and external fields require additional angular momenta and Hamiltonian terms.
Professor mode · Landé interval rule and audit

Adjacent ideal levels satisfy \(E_J-E_{J-1}=AJ\). The degeneracy-weighted shift must sum to zero over the complete J manifold. Both identities are tested directly against the production function.

Professor mode · the bridge to electronic structure

Real quantum chemistry replaces isolated-atom labels by molecular orbitals, basis expansions, antisymmetric many-electron states, approximate Hamiltonians or density functionals, and numerical solvers. Atomic quantum numbers remain invaluable for basis design, interpretation, spectroscopy, and asymptotic fragments—but molecular symmetry and state mixing decide what survives exactly.
