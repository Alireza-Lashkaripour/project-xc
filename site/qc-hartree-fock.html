Hartree–Fock-model boundary

Finite and variational does not mean exact molecular truth. These laboratories optimize one determinant inside declared two-orbital or two-AO teaching spaces. A small SCF residual certifies a fixed point of that model—not the global HF minimum, complete-basis convergence, spin purity, electron correlation, or agreement with experiment. The two-level SCF map is a transparent nonlinear surrogate, not an ab initio molecular calculation.

A determinant becomes Hartree–Fock only after orbital stationarity

Level 2 · Coulomb/exchange forge

Direct repulsion sees charge; exchange sees same-spin antisymmetry

For a supplied orbital pair, combine \(J\) and \(K\) only after checking the spin relation. Opposite-spin exchange vanishes after spin integration. For one spin orbital interacting with itself, \(J=K\) cancels the mean-field self term.

Level 3 · Fock matrix foundry

The operator depends on the orbitals it is asked to produce

In the declared real spin-orbital convention, \(F_{pq}=h_{pq}+\sum_{rs}D^{(so)}_{rs}\langle pr\Vert qs\rangle\), where \(D^{(so)}\) is the full occupation-one 1-RDM over all occupied alpha and beta spin orbitals. Audit both integral permutations and Hermiticity; changing \(D^{(so)}\) changes \(F[D^{(so)}]\), which makes Hartree–Fock nonlinear.

Level 4 · Roothaan–Hall solver

The AO metric belongs inside the eigenproblem

For nonorthogonal atomic orbitals, solve \(FC=SC\varepsilon\), not an ordinary eigenproblem in raw AO coordinates. A valid solution must satisfy \(C^TSC=I\) and a small residual for every reported root.

Level 5 · Density closure lab

A determinant density is a projector in the AO metric

For one spin sector, \(D=C_{occ}C_{occ}^T\), \(\mathrm{Tr}(DS)=N_{occ}\), and \(DSD=D\). For the closed-shell spin-summed matrix \(P=2D\), the corresponding closure is \(PSP=2P\).

Level 6 · Brillouin stationarity gate

Zero occupied–virtual coupling is stationarity evidence—not a correlation theorem

At a stationary HF determinant, occupied–virtual Fock elements vanish in the optimized framework, equivalently \(\langle\Phi_i^a|H|\Phi_0\rangle=0\) under the usual Brillouin conditions. Occupied–occupied and virtual–virtual rotations change orbital representation without changing the determinant subspaces.

Professor mode · orbital invariance

Unitary rotations confined to the occupied subspace leave a single determinant invariant up to phase; virtual-only rotations leave the occupied determinant unchanged. Canonical orbitals diagonalize convenient Fock blocks, but individual orbital energies and shapes are representation-dependent.

Input density and output density must agree

Level 10 · DIIS residual mixer

Extrapolate error vectors, not a promise of lower energy

DIIS chooses coefficients \(c_i\) with \(\sum_i c_i=1\) to reduce the norm of a residual combination. Nearly redundant residuals make the small Pulay system singular or ill-conditioned.

Level 11 · Reference and spin audit

Choose RHF, ROHF, or UHF from the state and the evidence target

RHF pairs alpha and beta electrons in common spatial orbitals; ROHF retains common closed-shell orbitals plus open-shell occupancy; UHF permits distinct alpha and beta orbitals. For the declared UHF convention, \(\langle S^2\rangle=M_S(M_S+1)+N_\beta-\sum_{ij}|S^{\alpha\beta}_{ij}|^2\).

Target constraint: paired closed shell with common spatial orbitals and spin purity required.

Professor mode · broken symmetry and stability

A lower UHF energy can reflect useful static-correlation-like symmetry breaking, an undesired spin-contaminated solution, or both. Compare state character, \(\langle S^2\rangle\), orbital stability, geometry dependence, and the property being predicted.
