Approximation-model boundary

Exact means exact only inside the stated finite or analytic model. Variational bounds require a normalized admissible trial and the right Hamiltonian hypotheses. Perturbative ratios and overlap thresholds are diagnostics, not universal theorems. Signed computational errors can cancel and are not statistical uncertainties. Every laboratory names what was solved, what was omitted, and what evidence would be needed next.

Level 2 · Remove accidental units

Dimensionless scaling forge

For $H=p^2/(2m)+kx^2/2+\beta x^4$, choose $\omega=\sqrt{k/m}$ and $\ell=\sqrt{\hbar/(m\omega)}$. The scaled shape is controlled by $g=\beta\hbar/(m^2\omega^3)$—not by three raw parameters independently.

  3. Commit what the potential comparison still does not determine.
  4. Lock the design only when both numerical and reasoning targets pass.

Professor mode · Why scaling comes first

Dimensionless groups reveal the actual expansion parameter, prevent unit-dependent “smallness,” and make cross-system comparisons meaningful. Scaling does not create a controlled approximation; it tells you which combinations must be controlled.

Level 4 · Restricted trial family

Gaussian variational sculptor

For the dimensionless harmonic oscillator and $\psi_\alpha\propto e^{-\alpha x^2/2}$, $T=\alpha/4$, $V=1/(4\alpha)$, and $E\ge 1/2$. Narrowing raises kinetic cost; broadening raises potential cost.

Balance the width penalties

  1. Record a diffuse sample.
  2. Predict its reciprocal equal-energy partner.
  3. Record the narrow partner.
  4. Manually lock the virial-balanced minimum before any reveal.

Professor mode · Why nesting matters

If two spaces are not nested, a lower energy in the second calculation does not by itself define a monotone convergence sequence. Real basis families can also change other approximations, thresholds, grids, and linear-dependence treatments at the same time.

Level 8 · Degenerate subspaces

Degenerate-subspace crossroads

Inside an exactly degenerate subspace, first-order shifts are the eigenvalues of the restricted perturbation $W$. Its diagonal entries change when the basis rotates; its trace, determinant, and eigenvalues do not.

Remove basis-dependent guessing

Stages: diagonal basis 0 · rotated invariants 0 · identity case 0
