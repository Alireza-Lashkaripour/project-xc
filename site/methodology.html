Methodology

Formula and curation policy

Project XC separates broad imported coverage from curated scientific definitions. Every record has a formula panel, but the status label tells you whether it is an exact curated decomposition or a generic scaffold derived from rung/kind.

Formula status labels

  • curated-partial: Project XC has an interpreted formula or amount, but more primary-source review may be needed.
  • generic-derived: a generic LDA/GGA/meta-GGA/hybrid scaffold generated from classification, not a full implementation formula.
  • unknown-not-curated: a coefficient or parameter is not yet verified.
  • not-applicable: the channel does not apply, e.g. exact exchange for a correlation-only record.

Validation gates

  1. Imported Libxc records must have unique code/id and source provenance.
  2. Every displayed record must have a formula object with exact-exchange/correlation status.
  3. Curated numerical amounts must be explicit and must not be silently inferred from names.
  4. Program aliases require per-program evidence before being marked verified.
  5. External terms such as D3/D4 dispersion or VV10 nonlocal correlation must be labeled as outside/inside the XC kernel only after source review.

Known ambiguity classes

  • B3LYP VWN3/VWN5/RPA convention differences.
  • Range-separated functionals where ω and exact-exchange fractions are input-dependent or implementation-dependent.
  • Dispersion-corrected names where the correction may be outside the semilocal XC library.
  • Nonlocal correlation terms such as VV10 that may require separate grids/kernels.
  • Meta-GGA grid sensitivity and kinetic-energy-density conventions.
