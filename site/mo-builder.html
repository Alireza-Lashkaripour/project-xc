Interactive orbital sketcher

Paste a geometry and draw a qualitative MO diagram

This browser-only tool parses an XYZ geometry, infers bonds, builds a simple qualitative LCAO/Hückel Hamiltonian, diagonalizes it in JavaScript, and overlays each MO state on the molecular skeleton. It is designed for active-space intuition: which atoms/fragments carry the frontier orbitals, which bonding/antibonding partners appear, and what orbitals should be inspected in a real calculation.

Scientific caveat. This is not Hartree–Fock, DFT, CASSCF, or a substitute for a quantum-chemistry calculation. The π model is a Hückel-style p-orbital graph; the atom-graph model is an intentionally crude one-orbital-per-atom LCAO sketch. Use it to form a hypothesis, then verify with computed canonical, localized, or natural orbitals and occupations.

How to interpret the output

  • Energy levels are relative model eigenvalues. Their ordering is useful; the numeric values are not excitation energies.
  • Blue and orange paired lobes are opposite phases of the same local pπ AO. For planar π systems the AO normal is perpendicular to the molecular plane; the 2D drawing uses paired above/below-plane lobes to show that phase.
  • Green/red connection overlays show how mixed AOs combine: green means same-phase bonding across that contact, red dashed means opposite-phase/antibonding node, and purple dotted means weak through-space π coupling.
  • Degenerate/near-degenerate states are subspaces, not unique orbitals. If the page reports a group, rotate the displayed component with the slider and include the whole group in active-space decisions.
  • HOMO/LUMO labels use the estimated or overridden electron count. If the count is wrong, set the override.
  • For an active space, look for frontier MOs with large coefficients on chemically changing bonds/fragments, include symmetry partners, and include bonding/antibonding partners together.
  • If π-Hückel fails, the molecule may not have a simple conjugated p network; use the atom-graph sketch only as a rough map and inspect real computed orbitals.

Project XC MO builder is a qualitative educational prefilter. Publication-grade active spaces must be checked with real orbital calculations and natural occupations.
